The article discusses the release of a leaked draft report from the Robodebt Royal Commission, which reveals that former Labor leader Bill Shorten sealed a deal with the Coalition government to prevent a royal commission into the controversial welfare debt recovery scheme known as Robodebt. The report highlights that Shorten agreed to drop calls for a royal commission in exchange for the government agreeing to make changes to the scheme, which ultimately led to the program's abolition. The draft report raises questions about Shorten's decision-making and political motivations surrounding Robodebt.
